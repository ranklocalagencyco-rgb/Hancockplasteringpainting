Render does more than make a house look smart — it's the first line of defence against the weather. When it starts to fail, water gets behind it and the problem only spreads. Here are five signs it's time to act.

## 1. Cracks in the surface

Fine hairline cracks are common and often cosmetic, but **wide, deep or spreading cracks** let water in. Once moisture sits behind the render through a freeze-thaw winter, small cracks quickly become big ones.

## 2. Hollow or "blown" patches

Tap the wall. If sections sound **hollow and drum-like**, the render has lost its bond with the wall behind — that's "blown" render. It can't be painted over; it needs cutting out and re-rendering.

## 3. Bulging or crumbling areas

Render that's **bulging, flaking or coming away** in pieces has failed and is letting water track behind the rest of the surface. Left alone, sound areas nearby will start to go too.

## 4. Damp patches and staining inside

Failing render outside often shows up as **damp patches, tide marks or staining on internal walls**. If you're seeing damp inside, the exterior is a common culprit worth checking.

## 5. Green growth and constant moisture

Persistent **algae, moss or green staining** suggests the surface is holding water rather than shedding it — a sign the render (or the paint over it) is no longer doing its job.

## Repair or replace?

Not every problem means a full re-render. Isolated damage can often be [patch-repaired and blended](/services/plaster-refurbishment); widespread failure is better fixed with fresh [render](/services/rendering) across the elevation. The key is dealing with it before water does more damage.
